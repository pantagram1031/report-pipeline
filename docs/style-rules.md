# Example report prose rules

These are general defaults derived from editorial feedback. A project may
override them through its request or form instructions.

1. Avoid parenthetical English glosses after ordinary translated terms. Define
   necessary abbreviations once in prose.
2. Avoid repeated noun-ending labels and repeated parallel sentence openings.
   Connect claims into natural explanatory prose.
3. Introduce symbols and specialist terms where they are first explained, not
   earlier for decoration. Abstracts may summarize already defined results.
4. When using concepts beyond the target curriculum, either explain their
   function clearly or omit them. Do not name-drop advanced terminology.
5. Define technical vocabulary at first use.
6. Preserve accurate curriculum boundaries; do not simplify correct statements
   merely to make the prose sound easier.
7. After stylistic rewriting, verify that numbers, citations, equations, and
   logical qualifications remain unchanged.

These rules are examples, not a personal voice profile, and contain no private
corpus material.
